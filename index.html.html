The Company

What Strata is and what we believe.

Strata is the plural of stratum. In geology, strata are the distinct layers of rock and sediment that form over time, each one built on the one before it, each one telling a different part of the story of how something came to be. To read strata is to understand what created the present condition. Geologists do not stop at the surface; they go deeper to understand the structure underneath.

The same logic applies inside organizations. Different strata of a business operate under different pressures and with different information. Decision Overload often happens at the intersection of those layers, where competing demands from different parts of a business arrive without a system for sorting them. That intersection is where we work.

Decision Overload is a surface condition. What produces it runs deeper: competing layers of demand accumulated over time with no system for reading what is actually there. Before you can prioritize, you have to see clearly, and before you can execute, you have to know what you are building on. The name is a reminder of what the work actually is, because you are not just making decisions in the moment, you are also building something that will last.

Strata was founded in 2020 on a simple belief: leaders need a clear system for deciding what actually matters for their business or organization right now, and the focused execution to act on it.

We serve leaders of organizations and established business owners who have built something real and are navigating the complexity of what comes next.

The Research

The academic foundation behind the method.

Strata's research draws on real-life practice from advisors working with tech entrepreneurs on growth strategies, nonprofits developing community solutions, and organizational leaders navigating customer and operational decisions. That practical experience is combined with research across business, psychology, human development, and AI change management. The work starts from a specific belief: a leader's capacity to process information and make sound decisions is a business variable, and in a world of competing demands and constant change, that variable erodes in ways most strategy frameworks never account for.

McClusky's Theory of Margin is the primary research foundation. The theory describes organizational and individual functioning as a balance between two forces. Load is the total weight of demands on a leader or organization, including decisions that need to be made, competing priorities, operational pressure, and environmental change. Power is the total capacity available to meet those demands, including resources, systems, team strength, and the leader's own clarity. Margin is what remains when power exceeds load. When load grows faster than power, margin shrinks. When margin is gone, decision quality drops, execution slows, and leadership becomes reactive rather than intentional. Decision Overload is the name for that condition when it takes hold inside a business.

Additional frameworks from transformative learning and human development inform how Strata understands what changes during an engagement and what conditions allow leaders to build durable decision-making capacity rather than cycling back to the same patterns under pressure.

Most strategy frameworks address what decisions to make. Strata's research addresses what makes it possible to decide well in the first place. That is the distinction the method is built on.
